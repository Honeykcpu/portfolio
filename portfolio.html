Business Analysis Case Studies

Case Study 1: E-commerce Checkout Optimization

Objective:

  • Reduce the high cart abandonment rate (25%) during the checkout process for a mid-sized e-commerce client by redesigning the user journey.

Methodology:

  • Performed root cause analysis using Google Analytics data (exit points) and stakeholder interviews.
  • Defined new requirements focusing on guest checkout and simplified payment steps.

Key Deliverable: Requirements & Diagram

Documented the "Place Order" process flow using a **Use Case Diagram** to visualize actor (Customer, Payment Gateway) interactions and system boundaries.

Use Case Diagram

*Conceptual Use Case Diagram showing Checkout, Payment, and Authentication steps.*

Managed the implementation team through validation, ensuring the solution met the core requirement: **reduce clicks to purchase by 30%**.

Project Impact:

Reduced cart abandonment by 18% and **increased overall conversion rate by 15%** (A/B Test validated).

Case Study 2: Supply Chain Process Improvement

Objective:

  • Streamline the internal procurement-to-delivery process for a manufacturing firm, specifically targeting delays in raw material sourcing (Average lead time: 15 days).

Methodology:

  • Mapped the existing "As-Is" process flow using a **Business Process Model Notation (BPMN)** flowchart.
  • Identified three primary bottlenecks: manual vendor selection, slow internal approval chains, and lack of real-time inventory checks.

Key Deliverable: Process Modeling & Solution

Designed the "To-Be" process, automating the vendor quotation and approval steps via a Power Automate workflow. The flowchart clearly defined the transition points and decision nodes in the optimized supply chain.

BPMN Flowchart

*Conceptual Flowchart showing the To-Be, automated Procurement Process.*

The solution was deployed across the procurement department, minimizing human intervention and providing automated SLA tracking.

Project Impact:

Average raw material lead time reduced from 15 days to 12 days, resulting in **20% improvement in operational efficiency**.
